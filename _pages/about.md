Dr. Tanvir Arafin is an Assistant Professor at the ECE Department at Morgan State University. He received M.S. and Ph.D. degrees in Electrical and Computer Engineering from the University of Maryland, College Park in 2016 and 2018. 

Dr. Arafin’s research focuses on hardware-based authentication, memory systems, and distributed neuromorphic computing. His work has been published in several top-tier peer-reviewed journals and conferences, such as IEEE Transactions on Very Large Scale Integration Systems (TVLSI), ACM International Conference on Computer-Aided Design (ICCAD), and Asia and South Pacific Design Automation Conference (ASP-DAC). He won the IEEE Asian Hardware Oriented Security and Trust Symposium (AsianHOST) best paper award in 2018. He was a recipient of the prestigious A. James Clerk School of Engineering fellowship (2012). 

Dr. Arafin is an avid traveler with a lot of stories to share.
